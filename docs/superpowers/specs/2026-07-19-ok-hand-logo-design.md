# OK Hand Logo Design

## Purpose

Create a compact symbol for QwenChaNa Medias that expresses the Korean idea
of “괜찮아” (“it’s okay”) without relying on text or a mascot.

## Approved Direction

The logo is a symbol-only OK hand drawn as one confident continuous black
stroke. The opening formed by the thumb and index finger uses the product’s
vermilion accent (`#c43b2f`). The drawing may have a slight editorial
imperfection, but its silhouette must remain clear at 32px.

## Visual Constraints

- Flat vector appearance.
- Black ink and vermilion on the existing warm paper color (`#f3efe6`).
- No face, character, text, gradients, shadows, enclosing badge, or AI motif.
- No camera, sparkle, circuit, brain, or chatbot symbolism.
- Friendly and assured rather than corporate or cartoonish.
- Anatomically readable as an OK hand, including at favicon size.

## Exploration Sheet

Generate one image containing four closely related variations. Each variation
uses the same continuous-line concept while testing stroke weight, finger
spacing, circle treatment, and slight angle. Present the marks evenly on an
off-white background with ample separation and no labels.

## Selection and Delivery

The exploration sheet is for choosing one final silhouette. Final production
assets—transparent PNG and code-native SVG—are outside this first generation
step and will be prepared only after a variation is selected.
